# Identity Theft & SIM Misuse Response

> Educational, safety-first guidance for people responding to cyber incidents. This project is independent and is not a law-enforcement agency, law firm, bank, telecom operator, or account-recovery service.

A step-by-step guide for unauthorized SIMs, identity misuse, account impersonation, mobile-number takeover, and credential abuse in Pakistan.

## If the incident is happening now

- Contact the mobile operator through an official service center or helpline and request an immediate review of the number or SIM.
- Secure email, banking, wallet, and social accounts linked to the affected phone number.
- Check for unexpected password resets, call-forwarding changes, missing service, new accounts, or unauthorized transactions.
- Preserve operator messages, SIM packaging, service requests, account alerts, and identity documents used in reports.
- File a complaint with the Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA) for credential or identity misuse and report cybercrime aspects to NCCIA.

## Evidence checklist

- Phone number, operator, SIM/IMEI details where relevant, and the date service changed
- Operator complaint numbers, service-center receipts, and written responses
- Account recovery alerts, transaction records, and impersonating profile URLs
- Copies of identity documents should be redacted in public; provide full copies only through authorized private channels

## What not to do

- Never publish a full CNIC, passport, SIM number, biometric record, or recovery code.
- Do not rely on phone numbers found in unsolicited messages; use official operator and regulator channels.
- Do not destroy or replace the affected device before preserving relevant records.

## Simple incident timeline

Keep facts separate from assumptions. Record times exactly as displayed and note the time zone. Preserve original files; use copies for review or redaction.

## Reporting in Pakistan

For cybercrime complaints, use the official [NCCIA complaint portal](https://complaint.nccia.gov.pk/) or the current reporting methods listed on the [NCCIA website](https://www.nccia.gov.pk/). For immediate physical danger, contact local emergency services. For urgent financial loss, contact the relevant bank, wallet, card issuer, or payment provider first.

## Privacy and repository safety

This is a public repository. **Do not open an issue containing victim names, CNIC or passport numbers, phone numbers, financial details, credentials, private media, chat exports, case files, or other evidence.** Use official private reporting channels. Redact examples before sharing them for documentation improvements.
